Introduction to Dinosaurs

Dinosaurs were a diverse group of reptiles of the clade Dinosauria that appeared during the Mesozoic era, approximately 230 million years ago. They were the dominant terrestrial vertebrates for over 160 million years, from the Triassic period through the Jurassic and into the Cretaceous, until their extinction at the end of the Cretaceous period, around 66 million years ago.

These ancient creatures came in a variety of shapes and sizes, with some being the largest animals to have ever walked the Earth. While most dinosaurs are known from fossilized bones, they also left behind footprints, eggs, and even traces of skin and feathers, providing a fascinating glimpse into their lives.

The dinosaurs we commonly know today are divided into two main groups based on their hip structure: Saurischia and Ornithischia. Saurischians include the mighty carnivores like Tyrannosaurus Rex and the massive herbivores like Brachiosaurus. Ornithischians include a variety of herbivorous dinosaurs like Triceratops and Stegosaurus, many of which had unique features like horns, frills, and armored plates.
